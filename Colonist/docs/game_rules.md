# Rules and Game settings

## Objective
The first player to reach **10** victory points (VPs) on their turn wins. Earn VPs by building. Collect and trade for the resources
you need to build.

## How to earn Victory Points
Players can earn Victory Points by:
- **building a settlement**: 1 VP
- **building a city**: 1 VP
- **having the largest army (consisting of 3+ knights)**: 2 VP
- **having the longest road (continuos road of 5+ road blocks)**: 2 VP
- **having a Victory Point card**: 1 VP

## Game Board
- **19 hex pieces**
- Pieces are of type:
    - **4x Forests**: produce **Wood**
    - **3x Hills**: produce **Brick**
    - **4x Pastures**: produce **Wool**
    - **4x Fields**: produce **Wheat**
    - **3x Mountains**: produce **Ore**
    - **1x Desert**: produce **Nothing**
- Each piece (except Desert) has a number from 2–12 (excluding 7):
    - 1× each: 2, 12
    - 2× each: 3–6, 8–11
- **Ports**: 5× specialized 2:1, 4× generic 3:1

## Resources
- Each resource has **19** cards in the suply:
  - **Wood**
  - **Brick**
  - **Wool**
  - **Wheat**
  - **Ore**

## Buildings
- **Road**: 15 for each player
- **Settlement**: 5 for each player
- **City**: 4 for each player

## Development Cards
- Total of **25 cards**:
  - **14× Knights**: Move the robber to a new hex. Steal 1 random resource card from a player with a building on that hex.
  - **2x Invention**: Take any 2 resource cards from the suply.
  - **2x Monopoly**: Announce one type of resource. Each player must give you all their resource cards of that type.
  - **2x Road building**: Build 2 roads at no cost.
  - **5× Victory Point**: Play any number of Victory Point cards (even on the turn you build them) to win the game.

## Card Costs
- **Road**: 1x Wood, 1x Brick
- **Settlement**: 1x Wood, 1x Brick, 1x Wool, 1x Wheat
- **City**: 2x Wheat, 3x Ore
- **Development Card**: 1x Wool, 1x Wheat, 1x Ore

## Start of the game
Players are randomly assigned a turn order. The first player begins by placing their initial settlement along with an adjacent road. Each player take turn, placing first setlements and a road. Then in reverse order they place the second settlement and a road connecting the second settlement. After placing their second settlement, players collect resource cards corresponding to the hexes adjacent to that settlement.

## Turn Overview
A turn consist of two phases, which occur in this order:
- **Production phase**
- **Action phase**

Once a player has finished an Action phase and didn't win the next player start his turn.

### Production phase
**Roll Dice**: Roll both dice and add them together. This total determines which hexes produce resources this turn.

**Collect Resources**: Hexes with a number matching the dice roll produce this turn. Each player with a settlement on a producing hex receives 1 resource card of the corresponding type from the supply. If a player has 2 or 3 settlements on that hex, they receive 1 resource card for each settlement. Similarly, a player receives 2 resource cards for each of their cities on that hex. If there are not enough resource cards in the supply to fulfill everyone’s production, then no one receives any of that resource. However, if only one player is affected, give that player as many of those resource cards as remain in the supply.

**Resolve a 7**: When you roll a 7, hexes do not produce any resources. Instead:
- **Discard Resources**: Each player who has more than 7 resource cards must choose half (rounded down) of their resource cards and return them to the supply.

- **Active the Robber**: You must move the robber to a new hex. Steal 1 random resource card from a player who has a building on that hex. If multiple players have buildings on the new hex, you choose one player to rob.

- **Important**: A hex with the robber does not
produce resources when its number is rolled.

### Action phase

A player may take actions in this phase as often as he likes and in any order, as long as he have the resources to do so

**Trade**: A player may trade freely with other players and the supply to get the resources he need to build. During his turn, other players may only trade with him, not with each other or with the supply. There are three types of trades you may perform:
- **Trade with other players**: To trade with other players, announce which resource(s) you want and which resource(s) you are willing to trade. Other players may accept your proposal, make counteroffers, or make their own proposals.
- **General trade with the suply**: To trade with the supply, put 4 of the same resource cards into the supply and take 1 card of a different resource from the supply.
- **Port trade with the suply**: If you have a building on a 3:1 port, you
may put 3 of the same resource cards into the supply and take 1 card of a different resource from the supply. If you have a building on a 2:1 port, you may put 2 of the resource cards shown on the port into the supply and take 1 card of a different resource from the supply.

**Important**: You may not give away cards in any way, which includes trading matching resource cards (for example, trying to trade 3 ore for 1 ore is not allowed).

**Build**: Roads, buildings (settlements and cities), and development cards each have different costs. During your turn, you may
spend resources (aka “build”) to place roads and buildings on the board, as well as to take development cards from the supply. To do so, simply return the required resource cards from your hand to the supply.

- **Roads**: Roads are worth **0 VP**. Roads are placed on empty hex edges. A new road must connect to one of your existing roads or buildings. You may not build a road starting on the other side of an opponent’s building.

- **Settlements**: Settlements are worth **1 VP**. Settlements are placed on empty intersections. A new settlement must follow the **Distance Rule** and must connect to at least one of your existing roads.

    - **Distance Rule**: When placing a settlement, stay at least two edges away from all other buildings!

- **Cities**: Cities are worth **2 VPs**. Cities always replace settlements. To build a city, remove one of your settlements from the board, and place your city where the settlement was located.

**Development cards**: When you build a development card, draw the top card of the deck. Development cards stay hidden until used. They do not count toward the number of cards in your hand when a 7 is rolled, and the robber may not steal them. You may play **1** development card during your turn. It may not be a card you built this turn. You may play a development card before rolling dice or at any time during the Action phase. Development cards never go back into the supply. If the supply runs out, you may not build any more cards. You may not trade or give away development cards.

**Titles**

- **Longest Route**
The first player to have **5 continuous roads** in play receives this
title. If another player has more continuous roads in play, they
immediately receive this title. The Longest Route title is worth
**2 VPs**. When a player’s route is broken, they no longer meet the requirements for the Longest Route, the title is returned to the supply. It remains in the supply until a single player has the longest continuous route of at least 5 roads. That player immediately receives the bonus title and the 2 VPs.

- **Largest Army**
The first player to have **3 Knight cards** in play receives this title.
If another player has more Knight cards in play, they immediately receive this title. The Largest Army title is worth **2 VPs**.
